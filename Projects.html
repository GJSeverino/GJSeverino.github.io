Hysteresis in olfactory perception

The vast majority of artificial intelligence has focused almost exclusively on creating a machine that can recognize objects visually, or produce 'human-like' language. These primarily visual and linguistically oriented methods of understanding artificial intelligence have made sense under previous models of mind (GOFAI). Yet, if we are truly interested in prodcucing creatures that are capable of producing 'intelligent' -- or better phrased 'adaptive' behavior -- it is necessary that we shift our attention towards other forms of sensation and perception. Mechanosensetivity, olfaction, and gustation (touch, smell, and taste) are some of the most important, yet fatally understudied perceptual modalities. They are responsible for some of the most vital behaviors in considering a creatures evolutionary history, such as feeding, reproduction, and navigation. While there is only a single species that has shown langauge, it would be difficult, if not impossible, to imagine a being that could exist without some form of chemoreception or way in which to feel it's place in space.
